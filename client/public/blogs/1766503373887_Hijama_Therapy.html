What is Hijama (Wet Cupping) Therapy?

Hijama, also known as wet cupping therapy, is a traditional healing practice that involves creating suction on the skin using special cups, followed by gentle, controlled incisions to draw out a small amount of blood. This process is believed to remove toxins, improve blood circulation, and promote natural healing in the body.

Benefits of Hijama Therapy

  • Detoxifies the body by removing stagnant blood and toxins
  • Relieves chronic pain, headaches, and migraines
  • Improves blood circulation and boosts immunity
  • Reduces inflammation and muscle tension
  • May help with stress, anxiety, and fatigue
  • Supports overall wellness and balance

The Hijama Process at Dukhniwaran Physiotherapy

Our certified practitioners follow strict hygiene and safety protocols. The process includes:

  1. Assessment of your health and suitability for Hijama
  2. Marking and cleaning the targeted areas
  3. Applying cups to create suction
  4. Making tiny, superficial incisions
  5. Reapplying cups to draw out a small amount of blood
  6. Cleaning and dressing the area after the session

Is Hijama Therapy Safe?

When performed by trained professionals, Hijama is safe and well-tolerated. At Dukhniwaran Physiotherapy, we use sterile equipment and ensure your comfort throughout the procedure. Mild soreness or marks may appear but usually fade within a few days.

Who Can Benefit from Hijama?

Hijama can benefit people with chronic pain, fatigue, headaches, sports injuries, and those seeking detoxification. However, it may not be suitable for everyone. We recommend a consultation to determine if Hijama is right for you.
