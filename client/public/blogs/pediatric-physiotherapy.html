Pediatric Physiotherapy

By Dukhniwarn Physio | Updated January 2026

Pediatric physiotherapy helps children with developmental, neurological, and musculoskeletal conditions achieve their highest potential for movement and independence.

Common Conditions Treated

  • Cerebral palsy
  • Developmental delay
  • Muscular dystrophy
  • Spina bifida
  • Clubfoot
  • Orthopedic injuries

Goals of Pediatric Physiotherapy

  • Promote normal development and milestones
  • Improve strength, balance, and coordination
  • Enhance mobility and independence
  • Support participation in daily activities
  • Educate families and caregivers

Rehabilitation Strategies

  • Play-based exercises
  • Stretching and strengthening
  • Balance and gait training
  • Assistive device training
  • Family education and support

Benefits of Physiotherapy

  • Improved movement and function
  • Greater independence
  • Enhanced quality of life

When to Seek Help

If your child has difficulty with movement, coordination, or development, consult a pediatric physiotherapist for a comprehensive assessment and individualized plan.
