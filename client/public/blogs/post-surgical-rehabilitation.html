Post-Surgical Rehabilitation

By Dukhniwarn Physio | Updated January 2026

Physiotherapy after surgery is essential for restoring movement, strength, and function. A tailored rehab plan ensures a faster, safer recovery and helps prevent complications.

Common Surgeries Requiring Rehab

  • Joint replacements (knee, hip, shoulder)
  • Spine surgery
  • Fracture fixation
  • Ligament reconstruction
  • Abdominal or thoracic surgery

Goals of Post-Surgical Physiotherapy

  • Pain management
  • Restoring mobility and flexibility
  • Regaining strength
  • Improving balance and function
  • Preventing complications (e.g., stiffness, blood clots)

Rehabilitation Strategies

  • Early mobilization
  • Range of motion exercises
  • Strengthening and endurance training
  • Scar management
  • Education on home exercises and precautions

Benefits of Physiotherapy

  • Faster, safer recovery
  • Reduced pain and swelling
  • Improved function and independence
  • Lower risk of complications

When to Seek Help

After surgery, consult a physiotherapist for a personalized rehabilitation plan to optimize your recovery and return to daily activities.
